I study how education, health, and family shape life outcomes and inequality. My research asks questions like: does university education actually improve earnings and health, or do good outcomes simply reflect who chose to enroll? Why do siblings end up in the same fields? How unequal were the effects of the COVID-19 pandemic?

I work mostly with Swedish administrative data, combining sources in novel ways—for example linking archived university applications going back to the 1970s with modern tax and health records. I use quasi-experimental methods to isolate causal effects.

I am a researcher at the Institute for Evaluation of Labor Market and Education Policy (IFAU) in Uppsala, with a Ph.D. from the Stockholm School of Economics. I am also a research affiliate at IZA and CESifo, and I coordinate SWECOV, a register-based research program on COVID-19.
