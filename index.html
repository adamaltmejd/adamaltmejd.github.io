I am Adam Altmejd, a second-year PhD Student at the Department of Economics at Stockholm School of Economics, working mainly with behavioral economics and microeconomic theory.

I am interested in human behavior, and how it affects society at large - the macroeconomic consequences of humanity's limited cognitive abilities. I use evolutionary game theory to study how cognitive limitations could persist, and run experiments on the sensibility of behavioral elements in macroeconomic models.
