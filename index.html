I am a Post-doc at the Institute for Social Research (SOFI) as well as the Swedish House of Finance. I received my Ph.D. from Stockholm School of Economics in 2018.

I do research in empirical and experimental microeconomics, using econometric techniques to study how real-life decisions are impacted by psychological biases, information asymmetries, and non-standard preferences. My current projects focus on the realm of education choices and financial decisions.

{% comment %}

I am interested in how our behavior affects society at large - the macroeconomic consequences of humanity's limited cognitive abilities. Among other things, I study the role of peer influence on decision making, even in high stakes settings, and research how people form biased beliefs.

{% endcomment %}

I strongly believe that social science research needs to become more open and reproducible, which is why I am a BITSS Catalyst and partake in several replication efforts.

Publications

  • “Evaluating Replicability of Laboratory Experiments in Economics.” with Colin Camerer, et. al. in Science (2016)
  • “Using Prediction Markets to Forecast Research Evaluations.” with Marcus Munafo, et. al., in Royal Society Open Science (2015)
